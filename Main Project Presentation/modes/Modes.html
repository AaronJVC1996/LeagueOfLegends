League of Legends (LoL) is a popular online strategy game developed and published by Riot Games.
It offers a variety of game modes, each with its own focus and dynamics.
Here I'll mention some of the most prominent game modes:



Classic/Ranked: This is the main mode of League of Legends.
It's played on the classic Summoner's Rift map, where two teams of five players compete to destroy the enemy base, known as the Nexus.
Players choose a champion from a wide cast of characters, each with unique abilities, and work together to secure objectives, eliminate enemies, and win the match.

This map is used in more than one game mode, is the most popular map




ARAM (All Random All Mid): In this mode, players are randomly assigned a champion and face off on a single lane on the Howling Abyss map.
The goal is to destroy the enemy tower and ultimately the Nexus.
ARAM is faster and more chaotic than the classic mode and focuses on continuous team fights.

There is only one line to attack or defend, all players face each other in a single line
This mode charmed people who did not want to play very long games



Twisted Treeline: This is a smaller map than Summoner's Rift, designed for 3v3 matches.
Teams battle for control of two altars that provide temporary bonuses, as well as to destroy enemy towers and Nexus.
Twisted Treeline offers a faster-paced gameplay experience focused on action.

This game mode is the oldest, people usually play it more than anything out of nostalgia.



URF Mode (Ultra Rapid Fire): URF is a special game mode offered periodically and for a limited time.
In URF, champions' abilities have extremely reduced cooldowns, allowing for fast-paced and action-packed gameplay.
Players can use their abilities almost constantly, leading to frantic skirmishes and chaotic situations (Classic Map).

This game mode is not always available, they activate it eventually



Practice and Training Mode: League of Legends also offers game modes for practicing and improving skills.
These include bot training modes, where players can practice mechanics and strategies,
as well as tools like the Practice Tool, which allows players to test champions and builds in a controlled environment (Classic Map).

In this game mode you tend to practice combos, damage calculations, strategies, etc (Map 5vs5)



Killing Baron Nashor grants Hand of Baron Hand of Baron to all living teammates for 180 seconds, which grants Attack damage and Ability power

The Dragon is the second most powerful neutral monster on Summoner's Rift in League of Legends. Killing a Dragon grants the entire team the Dragon Slayer effect.

Vilemaw was the most powerful neutral Monster icon monster on Twisted Treeline icon Twisted Treeline. It served a similar role to the Nashor.

These are just some of the game modes offered by League of Legends.
The variety of modes provides options for different playstyles and experience levels, contributing to the game's enduring popularity.
